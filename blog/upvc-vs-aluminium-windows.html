UPVC vs Aluminium Windows: Which is Best for Your Home in 2025?

November 25, 2025 8 min read By Sai Innovation Team

Choosing the right windows for your home is a decision that affects your comfort, energy bills, and property value for decades. The two most popular choices in modern Indian homes are UPVC (Unplasticized Polyvinyl Chloride) and Aluminium windows. But which one is right for you?

In this comprehensive guide, we'll compare both options across every important factor to help you make an informed decision.

What are UPVC Windows?

UPVC windows are made from a rigid, durable form of PVC that doesn't contain plasticizers. This makes them incredibly sturdy and long-lasting. The frames are typically filled with multiple chambers for enhanced insulation and reinforced with galvanized steel for strength.

Key characteristics:

  • Made from unplasticized polyvinyl chloride (uPVC)
  • Multi-chambered profile for better insulation
  • Steel reinforcement for structural strength
  • Available in white, woodgrain, and colored finishes
  • Welded corners for seamless, watertight joints

What are Aluminium Windows?

Aluminium windows feature frames made from extruded aluminium alloy. Modern aluminium windows often include a thermal break ‚Äì a plastic insulator between the inner and outer frame sections ‚Äì to improve energy efficiency.

Key characteristics:

  • Made from extruded aluminium alloy
  • Slim profiles allow maximum glass area
  • Powder-coated finish in any RAL color
  • Thermal break options for better insulation
  • Inherently strong and rigid

Thermal Insulation & Energy Efficiency

When it comes to keeping your home cool in summer and warm in winter, UPVC windows are the clear winner. The multi-chambered profile acts as an excellent insulator, significantly reducing heat transfer.

Did You Know?

UPVC windows can reduce your air conditioning costs by up to 30% compared to traditional aluminium windows without thermal break.

Aluminium is a natural conductor of heat, which means standard aluminium windows allow heat to pass through easily. However, thermal break aluminium windows include a plastic barrier that significantly improves insulation, making them comparable to UPVC in energy efficiency.

Sound Insulation

Living near a busy road or in a noisy neighborhood? Sound insulation should be your top priority.

UPVC windows excel in soundproofing, offering noise reduction of up to 45dB when combined with double-glazed acoustic glass. The multi-chambered profile and fusion-welded corners create an airtight seal that blocks external noise effectively.

Aluminium windows typically offer 25-35dB noise reduction. While this is adequate for most situations, if you're in a high-noise area, UPVC is the better choice.

Durability & Lifespan

Aluminium windows win in pure longevity, lasting 40+ years with proper maintenance. They're resistant to corrosion, don't warp, and maintain structural integrity even in extreme conditions.

UPVC windows typically last 25-30 years. While they don't rust or rot, prolonged exposure to intense UV light can cause some discoloration over decades (though modern UPVC includes UV stabilizers to minimize this).

Important: Both window types significantly outlast traditional wooden windows, which require constant maintenance and replacement every 10-15 years.

Aesthetics & Design Flexibility

If aesthetics and design flexibility are your priority, aluminium windows offer more options:

  • Slimmer profiles: More glass, less frame ‚Äì perfect for modern, minimalist designs
  • Unlimited colors: Can be powder-coated in any RAL color
  • Contemporary look: Ideal for modern architecture
  • Large spans: Can support larger glass panels without compromising strength

UPVC windows traditionally come in white, though woodgrain foils and limited color options are available. The wider profiles give a more traditional look, which some homeowners prefer.

Maintenance Requirements

Both window types are low maintenance, but UPVC has a slight edge:

UPVC Maintenance:

  • Wipe with soap and water occasionally
  • Lubricate hardware once a year
  • No painting or sealing required ever
  • Clean drainage holes annually

Aluminium Maintenance:

  • Clean with mild detergent regularly
  • Lubricate hardware twice a year
  • Check powder coating for scratches
  • May need repainting after 15-20 years in harsh environments

Best Choice for Indian Climate

India's diverse climate ‚Äì from scorching summers to monsoon humidity ‚Äì requires windows that can handle everything. Here's our recommendation:

Our Recommendation

For most Indian homes, UPVC windows are the better choice due to superior thermal insulation (crucial for reducing AC costs), excellent soundproofing, zero maintenance, and termite/moisture resistance.

Choose Aluminium if:

  • You want a modern, minimalist aesthetic with slim profiles
  • You need very large window openings
  • Color matching with exterior design is important
  • It's for commercial or high-rise buildings

Final Verdict

There's no one-size-fits-all answer. The best choice depends on your specific needs:

  • Choose UPVC for: Bedrooms, living rooms, apartments near busy roads, residential projects prioritizing energy efficiency and soundproofing
  • Choose Aluminium for: Modern architectural designs, large openings, commercial spaces, areas where aesthetics are paramount
  • Consider both: Many homeowners use UPVC for bedrooms (soundproofing) and aluminium for living areas (large glass panels)

Frequently Asked Questions

Which is better UPVC or Aluminium windows?

Both have their advantages. UPVC is better for thermal insulation, soundproofing, and is maintenance-free. Aluminium is stronger, more durable, and offers slimmer profiles with more glass area. Choose UPVC for bedrooms and living areas, Aluminium for large openings and commercial spaces.

Are UPVC windows good for Indian climate?

Yes, UPVC windows are excellent for Indian climate. They provide superior thermal insulation, reducing AC costs by up to 30%. They are also resistant to humidity, termites, and don't rust or corrode, making them ideal for Indian weather conditions.

How long do UPVC windows last?

High-quality UPVC windows can last 25-30 years with minimal maintenance. They are UV resistant, don't fade, rot, or warp, and maintain their appearance and functionality throughout their lifespan.

Do aluminium windows rust?

No, aluminium doesn't rust. It may oxidize over time, but this actually creates a protective layer. Modern powder-coated aluminium windows maintain their appearance for decades with minimal maintenance.
