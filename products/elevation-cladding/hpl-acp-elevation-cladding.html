What is ACP Elevation Cladding?

ACP (Aluminium Composite Panel) is the go-to cladding material for commercial building elevations. Made with aluminium coil on both sides sandwiching a polyethylene core, ACP offers exceptional durability, weather resistance, and a sleek modern finish that transforms any commercial facade.

For wooden finishes, we use PVDF (Polyvinylidene Fluoride) coating ‚Äì a premium coating technology that provides superior color retention, UV resistance, and a realistic wood texture. One side features the decorative color/wood finish, while the reverse is plain.

Frequently Asked Questions

What coating is used on ACP panels?
Both wooden and plain color ACP panels use PVDF (Polyvinylidene Fluoride) coating which provides superior UV resistance and longer color retention. The difference is in the finish ‚Äì wooden colors have realistic wood grain texture while plain colors are solid. Both have aluminium coil on both sides with color finish on one side and plain on the reverse.
What is FR Grade B ACP and when is it required?
FR Grade B ACP has a fire-retardant mineral core instead of standard PE core. It's mandatory for Railways, Airports, Metro Stations, Government buildings, and large public projects. The installation requires special brackets ‚Äì GI brackets (75√ó75√ó6mm) with anchor bolts or Aluminium angles (50√ó50√ó50mm) ‚Äì as panels cannot be directly mounted on walls/ceilings in these projects.
Which thickness should I choose?
3mm: Suitable for smaller panels and indoor applications. 4mm: Most commonly used for commercial facades ‚Äì good balance of rigidity and cost. 6mm: Recommended for larger panels, high-wind areas, and where extra structural rigidity is needed. We'll recommend the best option based on your project requirements.
What is 4√ó4 grid pattern installation?
We install aluminium profile framework in a 4-feet √ó 4-feet grid pattern on the wall. This creates a strong, uniform backing that ensures the ACP panels remain flat and secure. Unlike riveted systems, we use wooden screws and silicon sealant for a cleaner finish and watertight joints.
What sheet size is used for calculation?
We use 4√ó12 feet (48 sq.ft) standard sheets for calculation. Available sizes include 4√ó8 ft (32 sq.ft), 4√ó10 ft (40 sq.ft), and 4√ó12 ft (48 sq.ft). Your area + 5% wastage is divided by 48 sq.ft, and the result is rounded UP to determine sheets required.
What brands do you use?
We source from top Indian ACP brands known for quality and durability. The specific brand may vary based on availability and your color/finish requirements. All sheets meet the quality standards for commercial exterior applications with proper fire-retardant cores.
