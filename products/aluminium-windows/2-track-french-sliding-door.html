Frame Material

We use 6063 T6 grade aluminium profiles, which are stronger than standard aluminium. This grade handles heavy glass panels better and resists warping in extreme weather. The profiles come in 1.2mm wall thickness, giving the frame enough strength to support large door panels without sagging over time.

Glass Type

The top arch panel uses fixed glass, typically 6mm or 8mm clear glass. You can upgrade to DGU (double glazed) for better insulation or safety glass for ground-floor installations. The sliding panels use the same glass options, with 6mm clear included in the base price. For example, a 10ft Ã— 7ft opening with DGU glass costs about â‚¹15,000-20,000 more than standard glass.

Roller Capacity

The imported rollers handle up to 100kg per panel, so even large glass doors slide smoothly. The weather gaskets seal gaps between panels, reducing noise from outside by about 30-35dB. This makes a noticeable difference if you live near busy roads. The handles are ergonomicâ€”easy to grip and operate even with wet hands.

Warranty

Frame comes with 10 years warranty against manufacturing defects. Hardware (rollers and locks) has 5 years coverage. Glass warranty depends on the manufacturerâ€”typically 1-2 years. The powder coating resists fading for 5-7 years under normal conditions. We recommend annual maintenance: cleaning tracks and lubricating rollers to keep everything working smoothly.

Safety Notes

For ground-floor installations, we recommend safety glass (laminated) and multi-point locks for better security. The doors meet IS 14846 standards for aluminium windows. Regular cleaning of tracks prevents debris buildup that can affect sliding. If you're installing in areas with heavy rainfall, ensure proper drainage around the door frame to prevent water pooling.
